I’m too tired to journal.

At the end of the day or during a bout of depression or anxiety it all feels superfluous. But as someone who struggles with mental health, I know it’s important to track things. To understand what’s working and what isn’t.

It’s hard to journal. Sometimes I don’t know what to say. Sometimes I don’t have the energy to even voice my feelings, or figure out what they are.

You know how they ask you at the hospital for what your pain is like on a scale from 1–10? I just want to do that for my days. How was my day, bad to good, on a scale from 1–10.

I don’t want to feel pressure to write too much. I don’t want to see a big box or big blank page that demands me to fill it out.

I just want to write the bare minimum about my day and not feel guilty or embarrassed if it’s barely anything at all.

I’m too tired to spend too much time overthinking my own thoughts.

I’m too tired to journal.
